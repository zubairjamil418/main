Proposed Site Structure

A straightforward journey from arrival to booking enquiry.

1. Hero & Introduction

[Your best property / coastline photo]

Strong hero image, short welcome text, and a clear call-to-action to check availability or enquire.

2. Photo Gallery

[Property, rooms, views, local area]

Showcase the interior, exterior, surrounding landscape, and nearby distilleries or walking routes.

3. What's Included

[Amenities & facilities list]

Bedrooms, bathrooms, kitchen, WiFi, parking, linen, and any special features like a fireplace or sea view.

4. Local Highlights

[Islay distilleries, walks, wildlife]

Tailored content for whisky tourists, coastal walkers, bird watchers, and those seeking quiet island life.

5. Availability Calendar

[Live calendar integration]

Manual blocking or synced via channel manager. Guests see open dates instantly.

6. Booking Enquiry Form

[Clean, simple contact form]

Name, dates, party size, and message. Straight to your inbox. No friction.

Recommended Platform

WordPress — the right balance of flexibility and simplicity for a self-catering property.

WordPress + Lightweight Theme

I would avoid heavy builders like Elementor or WPBakery for this project. Instead, a lean custom theme or a well-coded lightweight starter ensures fast load times, better security, and easier long-term maintenance. You get full ownership of your site and the ability to update text and images without touching code.
